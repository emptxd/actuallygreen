EU-27 CO₂ Emissions Over Time

Since 1990, the EU has reduced its CO₂ emissions by approximately 37.3%, from 4,635,248 kt to 2,907,204 kt in 2023.

Sectoral Emissions Over Time

This “race line” chart shows annual CO₂ emissions in the EU-27 by five major sectors from 1990 to today.

  • Energy: Fuel combustion for electricity and heat.
  • Transport: Road, rail, aviation, and maritime transport.
  • Industry: Manufacturing, construction, and processing emissions.
  • Agriculture: Livestock and soil management emissions.
  • Waste: Solid waste disposal and wastewater treatment.

Energy remains the largest source but has achieved the greatest cuts, while transport lags behind.

2023 EU-27 CO₂ Emissions by Sector

Bubble area represents total CO₂ emissions (kt) by sector in 2023:

  • Energy (49%): Power generation & heat production.
  • Transport (33%): Road, air, rail & shipping.
  • Waste (8.5%): Landfills, wastewater & incineration.
  • Agriculture (5.6%): Livestock methane & fertilized soils.
  • Industry (4.4%): Manufacturing, chemical & construction processes.

This chart highlights where the EU’s emissions are most concentrated, guiding targeted policy action.

Country-Level Emissions (2023)

In 2023, Germany led the EU-27 with 740,673 kt of CO₂, followed by France at 339,016 kt, Italy at 331,153 kt, and Poland at 315,780 kt. Together, these four countries accounted for nearly 60 % of the EU-27’s total CO₂ emissions.

Emissions vary widely due to differences in energy mix, industrial capacity, and transport patterns. Smaller states like Cyprus and Luxembourg each contribute only a few percent, underscoring the uneven distribution of carbon footprints across the Union.
